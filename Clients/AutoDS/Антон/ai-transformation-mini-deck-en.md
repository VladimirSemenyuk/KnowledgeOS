# Mini-deck: AI-native PDLC

Purpose:
give leadership a frame for discussing AI-native PDLC without directly selling a "problem and solution".

Tone:
AI can significantly increase delivery if the software development lifecycle is ready for the new speed.

---

## Slide 1. AI Changes More Than Tooling

AI adoption often starts with:

- AI assistants in the IDE;
- chat interfaces;
- agentic coding;
- automation of individual tasks.

But the key question is:

> Is the PDLC changing so AI-driven execution can be managed?

---

## Slide 2. The Opportunity: More Delivery

AI can increase delivery:

- write code faster;
- complete routine tasks faster;
- build prototypes faster;
- close backlog items faster.

But delivery growth becomes valuable only if the company can clarify, validate, and accept outcomes faster.

---

## Slide 3. What Changes in PDLC

AI accelerates the middle of the process.

That makes these things more important:

- task definition quality;
- available context;
- architecture decisions;
- result validation;
- risk management.

AI-native PDLC is software development at a new speed, with a management system around it.

---

## Slide 4. The New Bottleneck

AI shifts the constraint:

- at the input: product intent, specification, data;
- at the output: validation, QA, security, privacy, accountability.

If these areas are not strengthened, AI increases work throughput but not necessarily business impact.

---

## Slide 5. Where AI Impact Gets Lost

AI impact gets lost when:

- product input is too broad;
- data cannot be trusted;
- QA remains at the end;
- "done" is not a strong enough signal;
- ownership is unclear.

This is not an AI tooling problem.

This is a PDLC problem.

---

## Slide 6. What Mature AI-native PDLC Looks Like

Leadership understands:

- where AI truly accelerates delivery;
- where speed creates risk;
- who owns the outcome;
- how quality is validated;
- which metrics show business impact.

The goal is not more code.

The goal is to turn intent into validated outcomes faster.

---

## Slide 7. What Changes in the Organization

AI-native PDLC changes more than engineering work.

It affects:

- employee roles;
- ownership boundaries;
- the development process;
- the software delivery process;
- quality validation;
- AI risk management.

---

## Slide 8. Why an External Advisory Layer Matters

MIT NANDA 2025 shows that external partnership materially increases the chance of bringing an AI initiative to deployment.

- external partnerships: ~66-67% deployments;
- internal builds: ~33% deployments;
- reason: internal teams often underestimate process integration, adoption, ongoing support, and learning complexity.

Conclusion:
an external consulting / advisory layer increases the probability of not just launching AI, but embedding it into PDLC, roles, quality, and delivery.

Such a layer includes:

- working with leaders from Director-level to Lead;
- changing roles and expectations;
- adapting PDLC and delivery process;
- managing AI quality and risk;
- supporting implementation, rather than only giving recommendations.

## Slide 12. Leadership Takeaway

AI should not be treated as a layer on top of the current PDLC.

It should be treated as a reason to rebuild PDLC for the new speed.

Opportunity:

> more delivery while preserving quality and manageability.

Risk:

> scaling AI faster than PDLC can manage outcomes.
